4. From clickstream data, how would you identify performance drivers for a specific company?

Time Spent on site

Time Spent is a low hanging fruit in understanding user behavior, whether the companies website is content driven or product driven time spent translates to user engagement

Bounce rate

Bounce rate is important for every type of company, the sole exception being blog sites where the probability of the user to move on without clicking is high. To move on with a single page view would mean that the user is not interested in the product that the website is offering

Cont.

Pages/visit

Analyzing Pages/visit gives the company an insight into the level of engagement with each visitor.

Conversion rate

Successful Outcomes/Total visitors is an all important metric to identify and track the success of the companys online marketing strategy. All of the other metrics are supplementary to conversion rate i.e they help in getting the user to do what you want them to do.

Cont.

Customer behavior segmentation

We can segment each visitor based on where they come from,

  • Direct Traffic
  • Referring Sites
  • Search Engines

Analyzing each visitor category separately we can pinpoint seasonal or demographic behavior and target these visitors individually.

Behavior segmentation also helps in tracking campaign effectiveness. Identifying the conversion rates from each of the various streams of the advertising that the company has invested in can help to strengthen user acquisition and also increase customer engagement.
